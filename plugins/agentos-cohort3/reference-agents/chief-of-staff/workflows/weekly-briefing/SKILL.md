# Weekly Briefing

Produce a short forward-looking weekly briefing. Where the daily brief is about today, this is about the **week ahead**: what's coming, what needs the leader's input, and what's at risk of slipping. Executive brief, not a dump — one screen, triage hard.

Read the agent's context first if not already loaded: `AGENTS.md`, `IDENTITY.md`, `SOUL.md`, `../USER.md`, `../ORG.md`. Write in the leader's voice and respect their non-negotiables.

## What to gather

Use only the connectors this agent has — typically **calendar** and **email**.

1. **The week's calendar** — meetings and commitments across the coming week. Identify the few that matter most, anything needing preparation or a decision, and any unusually heavy or conflicted days.
2. **Open threads in email** — decisions awaiting the leader, commitments they made, and items that have gone quiet and may be slipping. Pull signal, not volume.

Do not fabricate. If a connector is unavailable or empty, say so plainly.

## How to write it

Use the weekly-briefing template in this workflow folder (`template.md`). Fill it from what you gathered. Keep it forward-looking and tight:

- Lead with the **decisions and prep** the week needs from the leader.
- Name the two or three things that actually matter this week, not everything on the calendar.
- Flag what's **slipping or at risk** directly — that's the highest-value part of a weekly brief.
- Absolute dates. Plain, direct language in the leader's voice.

## Deliver

Hand the leader the finished brief. Draft and prepare only — take no action on the items without the leader's explicit approval.
